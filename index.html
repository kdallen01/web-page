National Parks

Grand Canyon National Park

  • Location: Arizona
  • Established: February 26, 1919
  • Acres: 1,217,403 acres

I have been to the Grand Canyon multiple times. The vastness and colors of the canyon are worth the visit. I have only been to the South Rim but plan on going back to visit the North Rim which is 10 miles straight across the canyon but the trip to get there from the South Rim is a drive of 215 miles. Additionally, there are less services and visitors to the North Rim. I also would like to visit sometime in during the winter.




Redwood National Park

  • Location: California
  • Established: October 2, 1968
  • Acres: 108,400, includes 3 state parks

I have visited the Redwoods National Park only once but plan on going back sometime in the future. There really are no words or pictures that will do the park any justice. You need to experience the park for yourself. Additionally, for even bigger trees visit Squoia National Park which is in California also,.








Glacier National Park

  • Location: Montana
  • Established: June 18, 1932
  • Acres: 1,03,572

I have been to Glacier National Park multiple times. The park scenery is amazing and very relaxing. There is on main road through the park called the Going-to-the-Sun Road. You may also get to see some of the wildlife including moose, bear, elk, mountain goat, and bighorn sheep.








Historic Hotel

West Baden Springs Hotel

  • Location: Indiana
  • Built: 1902

The West Baden Spring Hotel is only about 3.5 hour drive from St. Louis. If you don't mind the drive it would make a great day trip just to see the hotel. When it was original built, it had the largest self supporting dome in the country and maintained that recongnition for 5 decades. One of the things about the building is that the architect thought about the expansion and contraction of the dome depending on the weather and set the dome on a roller ball mechanism.
I have stayed at the hotel twice. When you enter the hotel you do not see the dome in the atrium immediately. In order to get to the original entrance and the front desk you will need to walk through the atrium. Upon stepping foot into the atrium you see the really beauty of the hotel. The size of the atrium with the dome is just breath taking.
